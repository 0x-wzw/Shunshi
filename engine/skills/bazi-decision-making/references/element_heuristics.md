# Five Element (Wuxing) Decision Heuristics

Practical decision-making heuristics derived from Chinese Five Element philosophy.
Each element provides a distinct lens for evaluating choices.

---

## The Five Element Lenses

### 木 WOOD — The Growth Lens

**Core Question**: *Is this sustainable? Where are the roots?*

**Wood represents**: Growth, expansion, planning, vision, flexibility

**Decision Heuristics**:

1. **Root-Before-Branch Test**
   - Before expanding, verify root system
   - Ask: "What foundation must exist for this to succeed?"
   - If foundation absent, delay expansion

2. **Seasonal Alignment Check**
   - Wood dominates Spring (initiation phase)
   - Good for: Starting, planting seeds, strategic planning
   - Challenging for: Harvest, completion, reduction

3. **Resistance-as-Redirection**
   - Wood bends without breaking
   - When obstacles appear: redirect rather than force
   - Ask: "What is this obstacle telling me about the path?"

4. **Maintenance-Growth Ratio**
   - Healthy trees grow slowly
   - Ratio: 1:1 maintenance to growth effort
   - Warning: If growth exceeds capacity, instability follows

**Wood Decision Matrix**:
```
Growth potential     [high/medium/low]
Root strength       [verified/unverified/weak]
Flexibility         [high/medium/low]
Season alignment    [spring/early_summer/neutral]
```

**Go/Warn/Stop signals:**
- Go: High growth, verified roots, spring timing
- Warn: High growth, unverified roots
- Stop: Low growth, weak roots, non-spring timing

---

### 火 FIRE — The Action Lens

**Core Question**: *What illuminates? What's the transformation?*

**Fire represents**: Action, visibility, enthusiasm, transformation, communication

**Decision Heuristics**:

1. **Controlled Burn Principle**
   - Fire needs containment (fuel boundaries)
   - Ask: "What limits will prevent burnout?"
   - Define clear ending conditions before starting

2. **Illumination Quality Test**
   - Fire illuminates but also casts shadows
   - Ask: "What becomes visible? What remains hidden?"
   - Ensure critical (not just convenient) information appears

3. **Transformation Readiness**
   - Fire transforms matter (wood→ash)
   - Ask: "Is this a refinement or destruction?"
   - Measure: Will the outcome be valuable ash or just ash?

4. **Momentum vs. Substance**
   - Fire creates excitement (energy)
   - Risk: Mistaking excitement for viability
   - Test: Would decision survive excitement's decline?

**Fire Decision Matrix**:
```
Visibility need     [high/medium/low]
Transformation type [refinement/destruction/unclear]
Controllability     [high/medium/low]
Burnout risk        [high/medium/low]
```

**Go/Warn/Stop signals:**
- Go: Visibility needed, refinement appropriate, high control
- Warn: High visibility without substance backing
- Stop: Destruction likely, uncontrollable, burnout imminent

---

### 土 EARTH — The Foundation Lens

**Core Question**: *What structures hold this? Is timing right?*

**Earth represents**: Foundation, support, nourishment, timing, trust

**Decision Heuristics**:

1. **Container Capacity Check**
   - Earth contains and supports
   - Ask: "What must hold this decision?"
   - Capacity test: Can existing structures absorb this?

2. **Seasonal Timing Window**
   - Earth dominates Late Summer (harvest preparation)
   - Good for: Consolidation, harvesting results
   - Challenging for: Rapid change, new initiatives

3. **Digestion Before Action**
   - Earth transforms raw material to nutrients
   - Ask: "Has this been properly digested?"
   - Warning: Undigested decisions create system toxicity

4. **Trust Verification**
   - Earth represents trust (stability)
   - Ask: "Is trust earned or assumed?"
   - Test: Would you build a house on this foundation?

**Earth Decision Matrix**:
```
Foundation status   [solid/cracking/absent]
Timing alignment    [late_summer/autumn/unclear]
Absorption capacity [available/limited/full]
Trust level         [earned/assumed/testing]
```

**Go/Warn/Stop signals:**
- Go: Solid foundation, late-summer timing, available capacity
- Warn: Timing questionable, capacity limited
- Stop: Foundation absent/cracking, trust assumed, capacity full

---

### 金 METAL — The Precision Lens

**Core Question**: *What must be cut away? Is it precise enough?*

**Metal represents**: Precision, boundaries, reduction, clarity, determination

**Decision Heuristics**:

1. **Cutting Test**
   - Metal cuts—decide what to eliminate
   - Ask: "What is essential? What's decoration?"
   - Criteria: Cut if removal improves clarity

2. **Boundary Definition**
   - Metal defines edges
   - Ask: "Where does this begin and end?"
   - Vague boundaries → vague outcomes

3. **Refinement vs. Destruction**
   - Metal refines (ore→metal) and divides
   - Ask: "Is this constructive separation or damage?"
   - Test: Does this preserve value while improving form?

4. **Precision-Accuracy Check**
   - Metal enables precision
   - Danger: Precise wrongness > approximate rightness
   - Verify: What level of precision serves truth?

**Metal Decision Matrix**:
```
Clarity level       [high/medium/fuzzy]
Boundary definition [clear/partial/undefined]
Reduction need      [essential/optional/none]
Precision type      [useful/ornamental/harmful]
```

**Go/Warn/Stop signals:**
- Go: Clarity high boundaries clear, reduction essential
- Warn: High precision but uncertain accuracy
- Stop: Destruction likely, boundaries undefined, fuzzy clarity

---

### 水 WATER — The Flow Lens

**Core Question**: *What's the underlying current? Can it adapt?*

**Water represents**: Flow, wisdom, adaptability, depth, resourcefulness

**Decision Heuristics**:

1. **Path-of-Least-Resistance Check**
   - Water flows around obstacles
   - Ask: "What obstacles can I flow around?"
   - Sometimes the long way is the short way

2. **Depth Accumulation Test**
   - Deep water holds more (knowledge, resources)
   - Ask: "Is depth sufficient before deploying?"
   - Warning: Shallow water dries up fast

3. **Adaptation Capacity**
   - Water takes container's shape, maintains essence
   - Ask: "How will this adapt if conditions change?"
   - Measure: Flexibility without losing identity

4. **Penetration Analysis**
   - Persistent water penetrates stone
   - Ask: "What requires persistence vs. force?"
   - Strategy: Water beats rock over time

**Water Decision Matrix**:
```
Adaptability        [high/medium/low]
Depth/resource pool [deep/shallow/unclear]
Flow state          [clear/blockages/turbulent]
Persistence need    [high/medium/low]
```

**Go/Warn/Stop signals:**
- Go: High adaptability, deep resources, clear flow
- Warn: Adaptability present but identity unclear
- Stop: Low adaptability, shallow resources, blocked flow

## Integrated Application

### The Five Element Checklist

For any significant decision, evaluate through all five lenses:

```
□ WOOD: Growth sustainable? Roots verified?
□ FIRE: Transformation clear? Visibility appropriate?
□ EARTH: Timing aligned? Foundation solid?
□ METAL: Boundaries defined? Reduction constructive?
□ WATER: Adaptation possible? Depth sufficient?
```

**Scoring:**
- 5 clear → Strong alignment, proceed
- 4 clear, 1 concern → Qualified proceed, address concern
- 3 clear, 2 concerns → Delay until concerns resolved
- Fewer than 3 clear → Restructure or abandon decision

### Elemental Interactions in Decisions

**Generating Cycle (支持)**:
- Wood feeds Fire → Good planning enables good action
- Fire creates Earth → Action produces foundation
- Earth bears Metal → Foundation enables precision
- Metal carries Water → Precision enables flow
- Water nourishes Wood → Adaptability enables growth

**Controlling Cycle (克制)**:
- Water controls Fire → Reflection moderates action
- Fire controls Metal → Enthusiasm melts rigidity
- Metal controls Wood → Precision cuts overgrowth
- Wood controls Earth → Growth can deplete foundation
- Earth controls Water → Structure channels flow

**Apply in decision analysis:**
```
If excessive WOOD → Need METAL (precision/constraint)
If excessive FIRE → Need WATER (reflection/cooling)
If excessive EARTH → Need WOOD (direction/growth)
If excessive METAL → Need FIRE (warmth/action)
If excessive WATER → Need EARTH (structure/containment)
```

### Temporal Element Mapping

| Time | Dominant Element | Decision Type |
|------|-----------------|---------------|
| Spring | Wood | Initiate, plan, plant seeds |
| Early Summer | Fire | Act, launch, communicate |
| Late Summer | Earth | Consolidate, harvest, review |
| Autumn | Metal | Cut, refine, set boundaries |
| Winter | Water | Reflect, prepare, accumulate |

**Application:**
Use these as timing heuristics—what season are you in?
Decisions made in wrong season face headwinds.

### Case Study: Product Launch Decision

**Scenario**: Launch new SaaS feature in competitive market

**Wood Analysis**: 
- Growth potential high ✓
- Team capacity (roots) verified ✓  
- Market fit research shows adaptability ✓

**Fire Analysis**:
- Visibility needed to counter competitor ✓
- Transformation: Beta→GA (refinement not destruction) ✓
- Risk: Limited budget = potential burnout ⚠

**Earth Analysis**:
- Infrastructure ready for scale? Unclear → Investigation needed
- Timing: Late in quarter (Earth season?) Mixed

**Metal Analysis**:
- Scope must be cut to core features ✓
- Competitor differentiation clear ✓
- Boundary: Only enterprise tier initially ✓

**Water Analysis**:
- Pivot options if market rejects? Defined ⚠
- Financial depth for iteration? Shallow → Concern
- Team adaptability? High ✓

**Synthesis**: 4/5 elements favorable, Earth/Water concerns about
infrastructure and funding depth. Recommendation: Launch with
dedicated infrastructure investment and pre-committed Series A
discussions (fix Water depth before committing).
